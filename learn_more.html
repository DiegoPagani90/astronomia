Sun

The Sun is the star at the heart of our solar system. Its gravity holds everything in orbit and its energy drives seasons, weather, and climate on Earth. This image from NASA's Solar Dynamics Observatory shows a coronal hole (dark region), a source of fast solar wind.

Moons: None.

Earth

Earth is our home planet, the third from the Sun, and the only known astronomical object to harbor life. It's an ocean world with continents and islands, featuring a dynamic atmosphere that supports diverse climates. This iconic 'Blue Marble' view was taken by the crew of Apollo 17 in 1972.

Main Moon: The Moon (Luna).

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and closest to the Sun. It has a heavily cratered surface and a very thin atmosphere, experiencing extreme temperature variations.

Moons: None.

Venus

Venus, our nearest planetary neighbor, is similar in size to Earth but has an extremely hot and toxic atmosphere, primarily composed of carbon dioxide with clouds of sulfuric acid.

Moons: None.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun, known as the Red Planet due to its iron oxide surface. It has polar ice caps, the largest volcano in the solar system, and evidence of ancient liquid water.

Moons: Phobos, Deimos.

Jupiter

Jupiter, the largest planet in our solar system, is a gas giant known for its Great Red Spot, a massive storm. It has a strong magnetic field and dozens of moons.

Main Moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, Callisto. (95 total known).

Saturn

Saturn, the sixth planet from the Sun, is famous for its spectacular ring system. It's another gas giant with numerous moons, including the intriguing Titan and Enceladus.

Main Moons: Titan, Enceladus. (Dozens total known).

Uranus

Uranus, the seventh planet from the Sun, is an ice giant with a unique tilt, rotating on its side. It has a faint ring system and many moons.

Main Moons: Titania, Oberon, Umbriel, Ariel, Miranda. (28 total known).

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and most distant major planet orbiting our Sun. It's a dark, cold, and very windy ice giant, with the fastest winds in the solar system.

Main Moons: Triton. (14 total known).
